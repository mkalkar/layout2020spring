Project Overview


Context of the Class

In this class we focused on using methods of interaction design with the context of understanding health challenges. We focused on designing products and services while focusing on user experience. With an emphasis on health and wellbeing, we’re able to focus on what is best for the user to maximize their experience with technology and new products.

Project Overview

We wanted to answer this question: How might we create an enriching and meaningful experience at the intersection of nutrition and community for elderly adults?

In this project, we paired up with Crown Center to create a new service to fulfill the resident’s needs health-wise. To create the best possible user experience we immersed ourselves in their environment to better understand their needs and desires. Our group wanted to focus more on transportation and building communities to maximize residents' well-being.

Research on Senior Living/Secondary Research



Health and Well Being for Older Adults

The environment older adults live in seems to have a difference in the way they self report their health. Those who lived in supportive environments reported better health rates. Also, those who have low mental and physical health seemed to be greatly impacted by the environment that they live in. Adequate housing, neighborhood, and social support can increase self-rated health, resilience, and social inclusion. Older adults should also do some type of physical activity every day and need to eat a variety of foods as they need more different types of nutrients such as potassium and calcium as they age.





Volunteers

Studies have shown that individuals with inadequate personal resources such as low income were less likely to volunteer. To include a wider population of volunteers there needs to be increased flexibility in time commitments and they should be able to choose the type of volunteer activities they participate in. People were also more willing to volunteer when the community provided them with some form of compensation and recognized the importance of their volunteer work.




Crown Center

Crown Center hosts many diverse cultural events as well as events related to cooking, musical, studying, clubs, and exercise. They make it clear on what kind of events happen in the month with a flier and post all the meals that will be given there as well. They also have bus rides that take residents to the supermarket and events that are out of Crown Center were also listed for people to view at the beginning of the month. We also took a tour of their living facilities and saw how they were geared towards older adults as there were lower kitchens and sinks. There were also bathrooms with handicapped features such as railings and there were emergency buttons that people can pull in case of emergency around the apartment.

Programs that Exist Related to Health and Well Being for Older Adults



Meals on Wheels

This program works to help homebound seniors get the nutritional meals they need. The people who request these meals are more vulnerable to mental health issues, have anxiety about daily living such as falling and have health hazards. After their continued use of Meals On wheels, users showed signs of improvements in health, mental conditions, and felt a better satisfaction in life as they felt less lonely and worried about being home alone. And has the potentiality to help people decrease their hospital bills. There were also greater benefits in receiving meals by hand rather than receiving frozen meals.





Create Circles

Created by Moolani, Create Circles, is a non-profit organization that pairs up young volunteers to older adults in long-term care facilities to make creative projects. Their goal was to change the negative views on aging and to help older adults challenge themselves in new ways even as they age.

The Problem We Chose to Focus On


1)Issues with Transportation / Getting Food

Here is a quote from a Crown Center Resident after being asked if they cook for themselves and they began to explain how they get their food supply:

“I don’t go to the grocery store because the bus can only take 2 people with chairs at a time. I let other people take it because they may not have as many food options as I do.”

Residents also mentioned that they used services called Instacart food which brings food to their door and is really helpful for when they are sick and can’t go out, but the internet is necessary and they usually have to tip people.

Furthermore, our interview with a student resident volunteer informed us that some residents used a service called Call-a-Ride for going to places such as hospital appointments, but they had to wait long periods of time and it was not very convenient for residents. Thus, we wanted to address these solutions by creating a system where residents can transport themselves with more efficiency and care. Residents will be able to post their own available times and create their own events to get them where they need to be at the right time.

2)Creating a Social Environment in Crown Center

Here are some of the issues that Residents have pointed out:

  • We were informed through interviews that when going to eat meals down in the café, people regularly sit in the same tables with the same people and don’t really try to meet new people.
  • Residents also stated that there day to day life is pretty similar and nothing is really new.
  • There was also a language barrier between residents as some of the residents were not comfortable with speaking English.

Solution

We wanted to create a way for residents to meet new people and do activities together. Thus, we are giving them the freedom to create any event that is open to all the residents on Crown Center. They can go to the grocery store together, or shop at the mall together, go to the zoo, or even go to the hair salon together. We wanted to create a system where a person can find a buddy by just doing things they would do in their everyday life.

How PickMe Works


Site Map




Sign In/Sign Up


Before viewing the PickMe website, a resident or volunteer must sign in or make an account to continue. For new volunteers, they will be redirected to a page where they would need to turn in important paperwork and fill out a submission form so that they can be approved to be a volunteer for Crown Center. Residents will just have to create an id, a password, and confirm that they are a resident by providing their information.



Request an Event Page


This is where a resident or volunteer can request an event.

Here is how it works:

1)Events are first requested using this request form.

2)If a request is approved, it will show up on the Sign Up page. Events will occur if one or more person and a volunteer join your event. For an event created by a volunteer at least 2 residents must sign up for the event to occur.

If an event request is private, it means that the event is very specific to an individual resident, such as a doctor’s appointment or funeral. These events residents won’t need more people to sign up and will just need a volunteer.



Sign Up Page


Here is your sign up page. Here both residents and Volunteers can sign up for events. Keep in mind that private events will only be displayed for volunteers and will contain the following icon:

Each Event shows how many spots are left, the date of the event, and the time.

Events also show if someone in the group has allergies or is handicapped. This ensures that everyone’s needs are met.

At any point you can click on details of the event, or details about someone. Do this by clicking the arrow by the event, or by clicking someone’s name.





Inbox Page


Here is your Inbox where updates on your events and status of your event requests will be.

Messages in your Inbox that are highlighted

blue show that you are currently viewing that message

Messages with the open envelope show that your message has already been viewed, and messages with the closed envelope have not been viewed.

This system makes updates very easy. When you get a message in your inbox, you will be notified by phone and email.

This makes event planning easy because everything is all in one place.



Home Page


Here is your home page. From here you can view your upcoming events, events you created, and profile information.

Events that are in green are events that have enough participants, including a volunteer.

Events in yellow show an event that still needs another participant or volunteer. These events may be cancelled.

You can edit your profile information at anytime. phone and email reminders ensure that residents don’t miss their event.

Some Unanswered Questions




How do we assure the volunteers come to the events and do not back out last minute?

We were thinking of creating a point system for each volunteer and when they would not show up to an event without a 24-hour notice there rating would go down. After a couple of warnings, they will lose their ability to be a volunteer for a set amount of time.




What if people want to go to an event alone or wants to recruit a volunteer for an event but not have all the residents see the event (Ex) Going to a Funeral, a surprise birthday party, etc)?

We are thinking of enabling people to request private events. So that people do not exploit having an event only for themselves, Crown Center will check the form and if approved they will pair them up with a volunteer to do their task. Residents may also be able to request certain volunteers so that they can meet a volunteer again when they had a nice experience with them the last time.





Does a volunteer have to stay for the full duration of the event (Ex) pick up and drop off?)

We recommend for the volunteers to stay with the residents if the event is not as personal such as shopping for groceries so that they are able to help them during the event and bond with them since PIckMe is to encourage community engagement. However, for longer appointments and personal events, volunteers may come back after the appointment is done to drop them off at Crown Center.




When a resident needs special care how do we make sure they will be helped properly by volunteers?

For the case for people with wheelchairs, volunteers who have big enough cars will only be qualified to volunteer for that event. Other special needs will also be considered and only volunteers who are eligible to accompany the resident will be able to volunteer for that event. Events with someone with special needs will be marked and made clear that they require more alertness from the volunteer.





Scopes and Constraints



We needed to consider options to when people would not own a digital device and how to contact them about sudden changes in the events or just to remind them of details. From knowledge of our own grandparents/interviews/background information, we were able to realize that seniors have a wide range of technology that they own. Some own phones, some have smartphones, some are comfortable using a laptop, and some do not own any.

To account for a variety of technology resources, we decided to send text messages/ calls/ and emails. Rather than our initial idea of having a Crown Center worker call everyone on the event list, we thought that this would be more efficient and take less time.

From secondary research, we also learned that more and more seniors have become comfortable with the use of technology and thought these technology-based reminders will be efficient to alert them about any details about their events. Technology availability also seemed to vary depending on their income and financial status so for residents who did not have internet access so we opted for a more traditional sign-up sheet (analog).




Process and Setbacks



  • Visited Crown Center to gather basic info

  • Researched senior livings and interviewed residents

  • Based on research and interviews, we discovered the problem with transportation.

  • We brought in different parties to fulfill different needs.

  • Approached in a digital and analog way, so that it can be used by many people.

  • Based on feedback, we re-shaped our service.


When visiting Crown Center to do the interviews, we learned a lot about how to develop interview questions, how to conduct an interview, organizing the information we received, and simplifying the content. These were all very new things for us, but it was a very enjoyable process to learn about different viewpoints.


It was also a bit hard to get in contact with one of the people we interviewed, she did not receive text messages and could only take calls. Also when trying to match schedules it was difficult to find a good time to meet since it was hard to match up with her busy schedule.


After talking with the people from Crown Center and having a look at what other teams had found, we dissected down the main problems that we wanted to solve for residents which was transportation and building a community amongst residents. We were unable to account for all the issues and only focused on these two problems since it was a reoccurring thing that seemed to be mentioned when doing the interviews.


Also, we began to conduct solutions to approach a wide variety of technology owners since older adults seemed to have a wide range in the devices that they own. So we developed both a digital and analogue way of using PickMe. We also had a bit of trouble coming up with how we were to contact the residents on the event list since there was such a variety of devices they might have been using. It would have been nice to know the age population and what kinds of device people from Crown Center to develop this part of our project.


After creating the wireframes, storyboards, and personas for PickMe we had gotten feedback on details that we did not notice such as how residents would communicate with volunteers when there is an emergency and how people will accommodate for handicaps/wheelchairs when in a vehicle. There were also suggestions for us to create a rating system and limiting their privileges as volunteers so that they feel more responsible to show up for the events they signed up for. Crown Center and students f rom other teams also suggested that we should also consider one-person events so we developed the private event feature for people to use.


We also had a bit of trouble figuring out what the progress is like to process events and check the volunteers since we were not sure of what Crown Center’s community guidelines were and how events should be processed. Since we were not really familiar with AI and how algorithms work to simplify data into categories for filtration, we were going to just have a Crown Center resident go through each event, but after some discussion, we realized that this would be a lot of work and time-consuming.


Lessons Learned Through This Project


Lesson 1

During the beginning stages of this assignment, we were able to learn about how to gather information through interviews, background information, and personal experience to understand out target audience. A lot of research was needed before trying to tackle our main issue of how to create a meaningful community for elderly adults since this was a population we were not familiar with.

Lesson 2

In the beginning, we wanted to include all of the residents. However, we realized it’s impossible to meet everyone’s needs, so we narrowed it down and focused more on a thing that we wanted to help. We learned which part to focus on and which part to give up.

Lesson 3

Transferring an idea of PickMe into a visual representation was challenging. We had to think eeper and more thoroughly to include every feature we wanted. In this process, we missed many things. After receiving feedback, we had to think of an efficient way to transfer our thoughts. Our key to overcoming this hardship was communication. We talked to each other a lot through text messages and zoom meetings. We discussed how to improve the model and easy access to communication was very helpful.
